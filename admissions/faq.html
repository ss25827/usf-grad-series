  • What are the graduate admission requirements for MIS?

    Eligibility Requirements

    Incoming students are expected to have the following as prerequisites:

    • Minimum Grade Point Average equivalent to B or 3.00
    • One semester of a high-level, object oriented programming language (e.g., C#, C++, Java) or substantial programming experience
    • One semester of Information Systems Analysis and Design or equivalent experience
    • One semester of Database Systems or equivalent
    • A course in Statistics
    • A course in Economics
    • A course in Financial Accounting

    These required prerequisite courses may be taken concurrently with courses in the MS/MIS program. Prerequisite courses do not count toward the 33 credit hours of course requirements in the MS/MIS program.

  • Do I need work experience in order to qualify for admissions?

    While work experience is not required for admission to the Graduate Series MIS program, it is highly recommended.

  • What does the admissions committee for the MIS Graduate Series program look for?

    The admissions committee reviews each applicant’s undergraduate performance, GMAT or GRE scores, work experience, letters of recommendation, a statement of purpose essay, and extracurricular involvement.

  • What is the process to register both as an undergraduate and graduate student?

    Students will not be able to register as both undergraduate and graduate students at the same time and for the same term. Students are advised to complete pre-requisites as non-degree seeking students and apply for admission as degree seeking.

  • What are the average GRE/GMAT scores?

    For the student applying in 2011, the average GRE score of accepted students was 1194, while the average GMAT score was 583.

  • Is there a preference for either the GRE or GMAT?

    No preference of exam, either will work for consideration of admission to the program.

  • I took the GRE/GMAT a few years ago. Will I have to retake the test? Can I get the GRE/GMAT requirements waived?

    Scores are only valid for 5 years. If you took the test more than 5 years ago, you will need to retake the test. The MS/MIS department seldom waives the GRE/GMAT requirement.

  • Can the GRE requirement be waived for students with an MIS degree and work experience in IT?

    Only in exceptional cases when a Senior IT Director or higher is seeking admission to the program.

  • What is the application process for the MIS Graduate Series program?

    Applicants should complete the following steps:

    1. Submit Graduate School Online Application.
    2. Provide official college transcripts (copies may be used to expedite the application process)
    3. Submit official test scores – GRE or GMAT (copies may be used to expedite the application process)
    4. Demonstrate proficiency in English, TOEFL or IELTS, if applicable
    5. Pay $30.00 application fee.
    6. Attach the following supplemental documents to your Graduate School Application (or fax to 813-905-9866 or email lisaorr@usf.edu ):
      • Resume
      • Statement of Purpose – Include your career goals and how the MIS program will help you accomplish those goals
      • 3 Letters of Recommendation – References should be from professors or employers

  • How much is the application fee?

    All applicants are required to pay an application fee of $30.00. All application fees submitted are nonrefundable.

  • When will my graduate application be processed?

    Applications will be processed when all application materials and supplemental documents have been received.

    Applicants are strongly encouraged to submit all application materials and supplemental documents to avoid any delays in application processing and to expedite admissions decisions.

  • Where do I send my official test scores?

    All test scores should be sent directly to the Graduate Admissions Office. For example:

    GRE: Test scores earned within five (5) years of the desired term of entry. Contact Educational Testing Services (ETS) to have the official scores sent to USF. The institution code for USF is 5828 and applies to all tests administered by ETS.

    or

    GMAT: Test scores earned within five (5) years of the desired term of entry. Official scores must be submitted to USF directly from the Pearson VUE Testing Service.

    TOEFL: Applicants whose native language is not English or who have not earned a degree in the United States must also submit TOEFL scores earned within two (2) years of the desired term of entry. A minimum total score of 79 on the internet-based test, 213 on the computer-based test, or 550 on the paper-based test are required. Applications submitted with TOEFL scores that do not meet the minimum requirements will be denied with no exceptions. Contact Educational Testing Services (ETS) to have the official scores sent to USF.

  • What is USF's Accreditation?

    The Southern Association of Colleges and Schools/Commission on Colleges (SACS-CC) accredited the University of South Florida in 1965 and reaffirmed in 1994. USF awards degrees at the baccalaureate, masters, specialist, and doctoral levels including Doctor of Medicine.

  • Will the degree earned be AACSB accredited?

    Yes, the degree earned is AACSB accredited.

  • How many transcripts do I need to send?

    One (1) official transcript from all institutions of higher learning where the applicant has earned a degree is required, but applicants may provide unofficial copies of transcripts to expedite the processing of their applications. Any admissions granted using unofficial transcripts will not be finalized until official transcripts are received in a sealed envelope from the Office of the Registrar where they attended. All transcripts must be in English; it is the applicant's responsibility to have transcripts translated and evaluated* before submitting them as part of their graduate application packet. If you are applying while still completing an undergraduate degree, you must submit transcripts of at least six (6) semesters of completed undergraduate work. Final transcripts showing the award of a bachelors degree will be required if an applicant is admitted and enrolls. Do not submit USF transcripts if you have attended as a USF degree-seeking student.

    *All foreign transcripts that are not in English must be accompanied by a certified English translation. Documents signed by a notary or other public official with no educational affiliation will not be accepted. Some graduate programs require a course-by-course evaluation. In the event that the University receives documentation that is questionable, or suspicious in any way, the University will require a course-by-course evaluation from a foreign transcript evaluation service. Refer to the Graduate Admissions' website for a list of evaluation services.

  • How do I withdraw from courses?

    Because this is a cohort-based program, withdrawing from courses will result in your being dropped from the current cohort. For information on the process to withdraw from courses, please contact your Student Success Specialist.

  • What additional information do International Students need to submit?

    TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language)
    Applicants whose native language is not English or who have not earned a degree in the United States must also submit TOEFL scores earned within two (2) years of the desired term of entry. A minimum total score of 79 on the Internet-based test, 213 on the computer-based test, or 550 on the paper-based test is required. Applications submitted with TOEFL scores that do not meet the minimum requirements will be denied with no exceptions.

    The TOEFL requirement may be waived if the applicant meets one of the following conditions:

    • Has scored 500 or higher on the GRE Verbal Test
    • Has earned a college degree at a U.S. institution of higher learning
    • Has earned a college degree from an institution whose language of instruction is English (must be noted on the transcript).
    • Has scored 6.5 on International English Language Testing System (IELTS).

    Bachelors Degree Equivalence for International Applicants

  • What order should IT pre-requisites be taken to stay on track and understand the graduate coursework?

    In order to understand the coursework and stay on track, IT prerequisites should be taken in the following order:

    I. Systems Analysis and Design
    USF - ISM 3113 or ISM 6123 (note: ISM6123 cannot be used as a MS MIS elective)

    II. Database Management
    USF - ISM 4212 or ISM 6217 (note: ISM6217 cannot be used as a MS MIS elective)

    III. Object Oriented Programming – C++, C# or JAVA
    USF - ISM 3232 or ISM 4141
    SPC - C# or Java
